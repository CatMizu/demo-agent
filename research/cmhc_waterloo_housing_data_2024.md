### Methodology Notes
- **Vacancy Rate**: Percentage of rental apartment units that are vacant and available for immediate occupancy
- **Average Rent**: Based on units in structures with 3+ rental units in privately-initiated buildings
- **Housing Starts**: Number of housing units on which construction has begun during a specific time period
- **MLS Price**: Average sale price of properties sold through Multiple Listing Service

## KEY INSIGHTS AND TRENDS

### Rental Market Trends
1. **Loosening Market**: Vacancy rates have nearly tripled from 1.5% (2023) to 4.1% (2025)
2. **Affordability Improvement**: Higher vacancy rates leading to slower rent growth and some improvement in rental affordability
3. **Supply Response**: Increased purpose-built rental construction contributing to higher vacancy rates

### Housing Market Trends  
1. **Price Stabilization**: After declines in 2023, home prices showing stabilization in 2024
2. **Historical Context**: 2019-2021 forecasts showed strong price growth expectations ($500K-$600K range)
3. **Supply Challenges**: Despite increased construction, Canada still needs significantly higher supply growth for affordability restoration

### Market Outlook
1. **Rental Demand**: Strong rental demand continues but being matched by increased supply
2. **Construction Activity**: Housing starts remain elevated nationally but specific KCW data limited
3. **Policy Impact**: Government incentives and population growth changes affecting market dynamics
