# Statistics Canada Population Data - Waterloo Region

## Data Collection Date
January 2025

## Data Sources
- Statistics Canada Annual Demographic Estimates
- Region of Waterloo Official Population and Household Estimates
- Statistics Canada Census Data (2011, 2016, 2021)

## Key Population Growth Findings

### Recent Population Growth (2022-2024)
- **2022/2023 growth rate**: +5.7% (ranked 3rd highest among Canadian census divisions)
- **2023 population estimate**: Based on 2021 Census with adjustments
- **Growth ranking**: Waterloo ranked as 3rd fastest-growing census division in Canada (2022/2023)

### Historical Population Data
- **2021 Census**: Population data available with regional breakdown
- **2011 Census**: 477,160 residents in Kitchener-Cambridge-Waterloo CMA
- **2006-2011 growth**: +5.7% growth rate (26,000+ new residents)
- **Long-term trend**: Consistent above-average population growth

### Population Distribution (2011 Census - Most Recent Detailed Data)
- **Age structure**: Median age data available for comparative analysis
- **Household composition**: 51.2% of couples had children aged 24 and under at home
- **Housing types**: 55.8% lived in single-detached houses, 10.2% in high-rise apartments (5+ storeys)

### 2024 Population Estimates (Region of Waterloo)
- **Methodology**: Based on 2021 Census with 4% undercoverage adjustment
- **Adjustments include**:
  - Occupied units to year-end 2024
  - Post-secondary student population estimates
  - International student population considerations

### Growth Drivers and Context
- **Post-secondary institutions**: Three major institutions contributing to population
- **International students**: Significant impact on population estimates
- **Policy impacts**: Recent international student policy changes affecting growth projections
- **Regional ranking**: Among top growth regions nationally

### Census Metropolitan Area Context
- **CMA ranking**: 10th largest CMA in Canada (2011)
- **Regional position**: Major growth center in southwestern Ontario
- **Urban vs. rural**: Concentrated urban growth in tri-city core

## Data Quality and Methodology Notes
- Population estimates include undercoverage adjustments (approximately 4%)
- Student population estimates are particularly complex due to temporary residence patterns
- Growth rates may be affected by policy changes regarding international students
- Regional estimates use Census base with annual adjustments for births, deaths, and migration

## Key Census Divisions Growth Comparison (2022/2023)
1. Highest growth region: +6.4%
2. Westmorland (N.B.): +5.9%
3. **Waterloo (Ont.)**: +5.7%
